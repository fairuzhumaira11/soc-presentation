Research Paper 2

"Ethics in Educational Technology Research:
Informing Participants on Data Sharing Risks"
Authors:
Marc Beardsley, Patricia Santos,
Davinia Hernández-Leo, Konstantinos Michos
(Department of ICT, Universitat Pompeu Fabra, Barcelona )
Published: 24 March 2019

Main Idea of the Paper

Main Idea of the Paper

  • EdTech research collects personal data from students & teachers
  • Most people don't understand data sharing risks
  • Just giving a consent form is not enough
  • Paper presents video + workshop method to inform participants
  • Study was done in 2 Spanish schools

Research Questions

Research Questions

  • What do teachers & students know about data privacy and risks?
  • Are they managing data safely and ethically?
  • Can a better explanation method help them make informed decisions?

Strengths of the Paper

Strengths of the Paper

  • Very relevant and timely topic
  • Real-world study in two schools
  • Practical tools like video & training sessions
  • Strong ethical message on informed consent
  • Clear structure and easy to follow

Weaknesses of the Paper

Weaknesses of the Paper

  • Only 2 schools → small sample size
  • No long-term follow-up
  • Self-reported data may be inaccurate
  • Limited literature search
  • No deep statistical tests

Conclusion

Conclusion

  • Teachers and students need better understanding of data risks
  • Consent forms alone are not enough
  • Communication model (video, training) is useful
  • Good guide for ethical EdTech research
  • Can help shape better research policies

Suggestions

Suggestions

  • Use this model in more schools & universities
  • Regular training for teachers on data protection
  • Follow-up studies to see long-term effect
  • Make consent forms interactive (quizzes, games)
  • Expand research to more countries
  • Involve parents in data awareness for students
